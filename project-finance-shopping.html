CarMax Product Strategy

Shop through Financing

Redesigning the car shopping experience to improve conversion for customers who apply for financing but don't complete their purchase

Role

Lead Product Designer

Timeline

12 months

Team

4 Designers, 12 Engineers, 3 PMs, 2 Researchers

Platform

Web, iOS, Android

Overview

Financing is a critical part of car buying for many CarMax customers. However, data showed that a significant number of customers who applied for financing with CarMax did not end up completing a purchase—a lower-than-expected app-to-sales conversion rate.

This project aimed to understand why financed customers weren't converting and design solutions to improve their shopping and purchasing experience.

Problem

Despite financing being a major part of car buying, customers who applied for financing weren't converting to purchases at expected rates. The challenge was to:

  • Understand why customers who received financing approval weren't completing purchases
  • Identify friction points in the post-approval shopping experience
  • Determine how to better connect financing offers to vehicle shopping
  • Improve the overall app-to-sales conversion rate

Goals

  • Understand the root causes of low app-to-sales conversion for financed customers
  • Improve the connection between financing approval and vehicle shopping
  • Increase conversion rate from financing application to completed purchase
  • Reduce friction in the post-approval experience
  • Design and test solutions in a controlled production environment

Process

Discovery Research

Conducted comprehensive discovery to understand the problem space:

  • Analyzed quantitative data on financing application and purchase patterns
  • Interviewed customers who applied for financing but didn't complete purchases
  • Mapped the current CarMax sales and financing process end-to-end
  • Conducted in-store visits to observe customer and associate interactions
  • Identified pain points and drop-off moments in the financing journey

Design Sprint & Ideation

Facilitated collaborative design sessions to generate solutions:

  • Ran design sprint ideation with cross-functional stakeholders
  • Created process maps and customer journey visualizations
  • Sketched multiple concepts for improving post-approval shopping
  • Developed mockups showcasing financing application components

Prototype Development

Built iterative wireframes and prototypes to test concepts:

  • Designed consistent car card components for use across multiple platforms
  • Created high-fidelity prototypes showing financing-integrated shopping flows
  • Iterated based on feedback from in-store research and moderated testing

Testing & Validation

Conducted moderated and unmoderated usability tests, then shipped a production-ready test in a controlled environment to validate real-world performance.

Solution

The redesigned experience focused on integrating financing more seamlessly with vehicle shopping:

  • Financing-Aware Shopping: Connected approved financing terms to vehicle browsing, showing customers which cars fit their approved budget
  • Consistent Car Card Component: Designed a unified car card that displayed financing information alongside vehicle details, usable across web, app, and in-store platforms
  • Clearer Application Flow: Streamlined the financing application process with better progress indicators and explanations
  • Post-Approval Guidance: Provided clear next steps after financing approval, guiding customers directly to relevant vehicles
  • In-Store Integration: Designed for use by associates in-store to help customers understand their options

Results

  • Improved app-to-sales conversion for customers with approved financing
  • Better customer understanding of how financing connects to vehicle selection
  • Reduced friction in post-approval shopping experience
  • Successfully shipped production test in controlled environment to validate design improvements
  • Established reusable design patterns (car card component) for use across multiple platforms and touchpoints
  • Informed future roadmap for financing-related features and improvements

Learnings

Key takeaways from this project:

  • Discovery is essential: Understanding the root problem through research prevented solving the wrong problem and ensured solutions addressed real customer pain points
  • Cross-functional collaboration drives success: Working closely with sales, operations, and engineering teams ensured designs were feasible and addressed business needs
  • Iterate based on real feedback: In-store visits and testing with actual customers revealed insights that desk research couldn't uncover
  • Design systems accelerate delivery: Creating reusable components (like the car card) enabled consistent experiences across platforms and faster future development
  • Test in production when possible: Real-world testing in controlled environments provides the most accurate validation of design decisions
  • Complex problems require patient, methodical approaches: Improving conversion requires understanding multiple factors—customer behavior, business processes, and technical constraints
