The Challenge

At Remitly, transfer issues requiring amendments arose from two sources: customer-initiated changes and business-initiated holds due to compliance, security, or data errors.

In practice, this meant:

  • 34% of customer support contacts were related to transfer errors
  • 71% of these issues could be self-resolved by customers
  • 41% of all transfer errors led to cancellation (~$4 cost each)
  • An additional ~20% of support tickets were proactive customer amendment requests

The Impact: These "killer events"—whether flagged by Remitly's systems or initiated by customers—created friction, increased operational costs, and significantly reduced retention. The existing amendment process required contacting support, causing frustration with wait times and complexity for issues customers could often resolve themselves.

The Solution

I designed Self Service Amendment – empowering customers to modify transfers independently with:

  • Amendment Hub: Centralized access point from transaction details
  • Opinionated options: Provide amendment flows specific to the customer's transfer
  • Eligibility Checker: Real-time validation of what can be amended
  • Guided Flow: Step-by-step amendment process with inline validation
  • Confirmation System: Clear communication of changes with email/push notifications

Results

Self Service Amendment delivered strong results across all metrics, and was called for experiment. We are excited to bring this step level improvement for our customers.

  • 65% reduction in amendment-related support tickets (exceeded goal of 60%)
  • 3.5 minutes average completion time, well within the 5-minute target
  • +18 points customer satisfaction increase
  • Successfully launched in 8 markets with full regulatory compliance

Learnings

Key takeaways from this project:

  • Autonomy with guardrails — Balancing user empowerment with regulatory compliance
  • Progressive disclosure works — Simplified complex scenarios without overwhelming users
  • Listen to support data — Support ticket trends guided continuous iteration
