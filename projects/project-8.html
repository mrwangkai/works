Role: Product Designer
Team: Indiana University Bloomington, Academic Project

The Challenge

University students face complexity when planning their academic paths. The project focused on simplifying course planning, schedule management, and degree progress tracking for undergraduate students at Indiana University.

Key Finding: Students struggled to navigate course catalogs, plan multi-semester schedules, and visualize degree progress—existing university systems were fragmented and not mobile-friendly.

The Solution

I designed The Plan—a mobile app providing an intuitive interface for academic planning:

  • Visual Degree Progress: Clear visualization of completed, in-progress, and remaining degree requirements
  • Smart Course Search: Searchable course catalog with filters for requirements, time slots, and instructor ratings
  • Multi-Semester Planning: Drag-and-drop interface for planning future semesters with automatic prerequisite validation
  • Schedule Builder: Visual calendar view showing course times, locations, and potential conflicts
  • Registration Alerts: Notifications for course availability, registration deadlines, and schedule changes

View the full project on Behance.

Results

As an academic project, The Plan demonstrated foundational UX design skills:

  • User research foundations: Successfully identified pain points through student interviews
  • Information architecture: Organized complex academic requirements into digestible formats
  • Mobile-first thinking: Prioritized essential features for mobile experience
  • Iterative process: Refined concepts through multiple rounds of feedback and testing

Learnings

Key takeaways from this academic project:

  • User research foundations — Interviewing fellow students revealed pain points I hadn't personally experienced, emphasizing the importance of research over assumptions
  • Information architecture matters — Organizing complex academic requirements into digestible, scannable formats required multiple iterations and user testing
  • Mobile-first thinking — Designing for mobile forced prioritization of essential features and clear visual hierarchy
  • Interaction design patterns — Learned when to use familiar patterns versus custom solutions for unique problems
  • Iterative process is essential — Early concepts needed significant refinement based on feedback and usability testing
