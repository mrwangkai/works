The Challenge

Remitly customers have a recipient-first mindset when sending money, yet our Home screen was transaction-focused. The existing "Send Again" feature was underutilized by frequent senders with multiple recipients.

Key Finding: 52% of customers sent to the same recipient but only 26% used "Send Again" – and 19% wanted to send to recipients outside their recent transfer history.

Goals

Increase next best action rate for frequent senders (11+ transfers), by making repeat transfers effortless. Specifically:

  • Increase transfer completion rate – make it easier for active customers to send again
  • Improve send again order rate – improve from 61.7% to top quartile performance (65.6%)

Target impact: $1.92M profit per quarter per 100 basis point improvement

The Solution

I designed Recipient Quick Send – a prominent shortcut section on the Home screen featuring:

  • Accessible: Horizontally scrollable recipient cards showing up to 10 recent recipients
  • Relevant: Smart ranking by most recent transfers
  • Ease of use: One-tap access that pre-populates the entire send flow
  • Business-centric: Strategic placement on home screen to match business needs and customer' mental model

Results

From the experiments we called for Treatment 3, which showed statistically significant positive results:

  • +0.55% profit per customer increase for frequent senders (Nth with 11+ transfers)
  • +0.66% completed transfers per customer, indicating increased sending frequency
  • 51.9% feature interaction rate among exposed customers, demonstrating strong adoption
  • $3.4M annualized profit opportunity from the feature improvement
  • Revenue per customer stayed flat or increased across all test segments, validating the hypothesis
  • Strong performance particularly among customers with multiple recipients, the primary target segment

Learnings

Key takeaways from this project:

  • Mental models matter — Recipient-first design drove 52% adoption vs. transaction-focused alternatives
  • Small lifts, big impact — 0.55% profit increase = $3.4M annual opportunity
  • Placement > features — Reducing visual clutter increased engagement more than the feature itself
  • Target strategically — Focused on frequent senders with multiple recipients (88% of top performers)
  • Ship iteratively — Used feasible ranking logic instead of waiting for perfect solution

Explorations

We figured out the what – a recipient-first send shortcut. Next was figuring out the where and how: what it would look like, and where it would live on the home screen. These explorations focus on visual design and placement options.
