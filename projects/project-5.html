Capital One Research Strategy

Service Blueprint

Mapping the small business card engagement journey to align product features with customer pain points and drive strategic roadmap decisions

Role

UX Researcher & Designer

Timeline

6 weeks

Stakeholders

Sales, Underwriting, Product Leadership

Deliverable

Service Blueprint

Overview

Capital One's Small Business Card team needed a strategic tool to ensure new product features directly addressed real customer pain points across the engagement journey. Without a unified understanding of user experiences across sales, underwriting, and small business owner touchpoints, teams risked building features disconnected from actual needs.

This service blueprint project mapped the complete customer journey across three user groups—sales, underwriting, and small business owners—to identify experience gaps and align the product roadmap with strategic opportunities.

Challenge

The Small Business Card product team faced critical alignment challenges:

  • No shared understanding of the end-to-end customer journey across different user groups
  • Difficulty connecting proposed features to actual customer pain points and experience gaps
  • Lack of a common strategic tool for leadership to communicate roadmap priorities
  • Multiple touchpoints and processes needed to be understood holistically
  • Need for a living artifact that could evolve as experiments shipped and insights emerged

Process

Discovery Sessions

Conducted in-depth discovery sessions with three key user groups to understand their experiences, processes, and emotional journeys:

  • Sales teams: Mapped their workflows, tools, and pain points in customer acquisition
  • Underwriting staff: Documented approval processes, decision criteria, and operational challenges
  • Small business owners: Captured application experiences, expectations, and frustrations

Synthesis & Mapping

Translated discovery insights into a comprehensive service blueprint:

  • Articulated user processes and emotional journeys across touchpoints
  • Grouped events into new categories to reveal patterns and opportunities
  • Identified key moments where experience gaps created friction or drop-off
  • Consulted product leadership to validate findings and prioritize opportunities

Strategic Artifact Creation

Designed the blueprint as a strategic planning tool rather than a static deliverable:

  • Created clear connections between experience gaps and product/feature opportunities
  • Built in flexibility for quarterly updates as experiments shipped
  • Designed for leadership communication and roadmap alignment

Deliverable

The service blueprint became a foundational strategic tool:

  • Journey Visualization: End-to-end view of small business card engagement across all user groups
  • Experience Gap Identification: Clear documentation of pain points and friction throughout the journey
  • Opportunity Mapping: Direct connections between gaps and potential features/products
  • Living Artifact: Quarterly updates tracking experiment impacts and evolving insights
  • Leadership Alignment Tool: Shared resource for strategy communication and roadmap discussions

View the full blueprint in Figma.

Impact

  • Strategic clarity: Created urgency and alignment around product roadmap priorities
  • Evidence-based planning: Ensured every proposed feature had clear connection to customer pain points
  • Cross-functional alignment: Provided common language and understanding across sales, underwriting, and product teams
  • Continuous improvement: Quarterly updates allowed teams to track experiment outcomes and adjust strategy
  • Leadership communication: Became the central tool for communicating product strategy and roadmap rationale
  • Reduced wasted effort: Prevented building features disconnected from real user needs

Learnings

Key insights from creating a strategic service blueprint:

  • Research as strategy: Service blueprints aren't just documentation—they're strategic tools that drive product decisions and organizational alignment
  • Multiple perspectives reveal opportunities: Mapping journeys across different user groups (sales, underwriting, customers) surfaced gaps no single perspective would catch
  • Make artifacts living documents: Building in quarterly updates ensured the blueprint remained relevant and reflected evolving insights from shipped experiments
  • Connect features to pain points: The blueprint's power came from explicitly linking every proposed feature to a documented experience gap
  • Design for your audience: Leadership needed a communication tool, not just a research deliverable—designing for that use case increased impact
  • Synthesis drives urgency: Visualizing the complete journey and its gaps created shared urgency that accelerated roadmap alignment
