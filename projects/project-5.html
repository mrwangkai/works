The Challenge

Capital One's Small Business Card team needed a strategic tool to ensure new product features directly addressed real customer pain points across the engagement journey. Without a unified understanding of user experiences across sales, underwriting, and small business owner touchpoints, teams risked building features disconnected from actual needs.

Key Finding: No shared understanding of the end-to-end customer journey across different user groups, making it difficult to connect proposed features to actual customer pain points and experience gaps.

The Solution

I created a comprehensive service blueprint that mapped the complete customer journey:

  • Journey Visualization: End-to-end view of small business card engagement across all user groups (sales, underwriting, small business owners)
  • Experience Gap Identification: Clear documentation of pain points and friction throughout the journey
  • Opportunity Mapping: Direct connections between gaps and potential features/products
  • Living Artifact: Quarterly updates tracking experiment impacts and evolving insights
  • Leadership Alignment Tool: Shared resource for strategy communication and roadmap discussions

View the full blueprint in Figma.

Results

The service blueprint became a foundational strategic tool:

  • Strategic clarity: Created urgency and alignment around product roadmap priorities
  • Evidence-based planning: Ensured every proposed feature had clear connection to customer pain points
  • Cross-functional alignment: Provided common language and understanding across sales, underwriting, and product teams
  • Continuous improvement: Quarterly updates allowed teams to track experiment outcomes and adjust strategy
  • Leadership communication: Became the central tool for communicating product strategy and roadmap rationale

Learnings

Key insights from creating a strategic service blueprint:

  • Research as strategy — Service blueprints aren't just documentation—they're strategic tools that drive product decisions and organizational alignment
  • Multiple perspectives reveal opportunities — Mapping journeys across different user groups (sales, underwriting, customers) surfaced gaps no single perspective would catch
  • Make artifacts living documents — Building in quarterly updates ensured the blueprint remained relevant and reflected evolving insights from shipped experiments
  • Connect features to pain points — The blueprint's power came from explicitly linking every proposed feature to a documented experience gap
  • Design for your audience — Leadership needed a communication tool, not just a research deliverable—designing for that use case increased impact
